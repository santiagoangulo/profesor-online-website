## Chapter 9. Derivatives and Applications {#cap9}

### 9.1 The concept of the derivative

#### 9.1.1 Average rate of change and instantaneous rate of change
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Derivative: definition from rate of change in Social Sciences contexts]`
Difference quotient. Average rate of change as the difference quotient. Taking the limit: instantaneous rate. Applications: marginal analysis in economics (marginal cost, marginal revenue).

#### 9.1.2 Definition of the derivative as the limit of the difference quotient
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Derivative: definition from rate of change in Social Sciences contexts]`
$f'(x_0) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x_0+h)-f(x_0)}{h}$. Calculating the derivative at a point from the definition. Geometric interpretation (slope of the tangent).

#### 9.1.3 Differentiability and continuity: the relationship between the two
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Derivative: definition from rate of change in Social Sciences contexts]`
Differentiability implies continuity (but not the converse). Functions with corners: unequal one-sided derivatives. Review of piecewise functions.

### 9.2 Differentiation rules

#### 9.2.1 Derivatives of elementary functions: complete table of basic derivatives
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Differentiation of polynomial, rational, irrational, exponential, logarithmic functions]`
Complete table: constant, identity, power ($x^n$), exponential ($e^x$, $a^x$), logarithmic ($\ln x$, $\log_a x$), root ($\sqrt{x}$, $\sqrt[n]{x}$).

#### 9.2.2 Algebraic rules: derivative of sum, product and quotient
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Differentiation rules, chain rule]`
$(f \pm g)' = f' \pm g'$, $(fg)' = f'g + fg'$, $(f/g)' = (f'g - fg')/g^2$. Examples of differentiating rational functions.

#### 9.2.3 Chain rule: derivative of composite functions
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Differentiation rules, chain rule]`
$(f \circ g)' = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)$. Identifying the outer and inner functions. Applications: $e^{g(x)}$, $\ln(g(x))$, $[g(x)]^n$, $a^{g(x)}$.

#### 9.2.4 Differentiating polynomial functions: extensive practice
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Differentiation of polynomial, rational, irrational, exponential, logarithmic functions]`
Term-by-term differentiation. Polynomials of various degrees. Applications: total cost, total revenue in economics.

#### 9.2.5 Differentiating rational, irrational, exponential and logarithmic functions
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Differentiation of polynomial, rational, irrational, exponential, logarithmic functions]`
Systematic application of the quotient rule, chain rule and basic table. Complex examples with multiple chained rules.

### 9.3 Applications of the derivative

#### 9.3.1 Tangent line to a curve at a point
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Applications: tangent line, relative extrema, growth/decay]`
Equation of the tangent line: $y - f(x_0) = f'(x_0)(x - x_0)$. Normal line. Economic interpretation: marginal rate.

#### 9.3.2 Monotonicity: growth and decay from the sign of the derivative
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Applications: tangent line, relative extrema, growth/decay]`
Criterion: $f' > 0$ (increasing) and $f' < 0$ (decreasing). Sign table. Application: identifying growth phases of a profit function.

#### 9.3.3 Relative extrema: local maxima and minima — first derivative test
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Applications: tangent line, relative extrema, growth/decay]`
Critical points ($f'(x_0) = 0$). Sign change criterion for $f'$. Classification as relative maximum or minimum. Applications: profit maximization, cost minimization.

#### 9.3.4 Second derivative test for classifying extrema
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Applications: tangent line, relative extrema, growth/decay]`
$f''(x_0) > 0$: local minimum; $f''(x_0) < 0$: local maximum. Concavity and convexity. Inflection points ($f'' = 0$, change of concavity). Application: complete analysis of economic functions.

#### 9.3.5 Optimization in Social Sciences contexts: profit maximization and cost minimization
`[Block: Measurement and Geometry (Change) > Applications: tangent line, relative extrema, growth/decay]`
Setting up optimization problems. Absolute extrema on closed intervals. Applications: optimal selling price, production level for maximum profit, minimization of per-capita cost.

### 12.1 Counting techniques and combinatorics

#### 12.1.1 Fundamental counting principle (multiplication rule)

The fundamental principle of counting. Direct applications: number of options in multiple-choice surveys, possibilities in a sequential choice.

#### 12.1.2 Tree diagrams: construction and reading

#### 12.1.3 Factorial: definition and properties
`[Block: Numbers and Operations > Counting: combinatorics techniques]`
Definition of $n!$ and base cases. Properties and simplification. Connection to permutations.

#### 12.1.4 Permutations (without repetition): concept, formula and examples
`[Block: Numbers and Operations > Counting: combinatorics techniques]`
$P_n = n!$. Examples: ordering candidates, scheduling meetings.

#### 12.1.5 Variations (ordered selections) without repetition: concept and formula
`[Block: Numbers and Operations > Counting: combinatorics techniques]`
$V_{n,k} = n!/(n-k)!$. Examples: forming committees with distinguishable roles, sports rankings.

#### 12.1.6 Combinations without repetition: concept, formula and binomial coefficient
`[Block: Numbers and Operations > Counting: combinatorics techniques]`
$C_{n,k} = \binom{n}{k} = n!/[k!(n-k)!]$. Difference from variations (order does not matter). Examples: selecting working groups, committees without roles, samples.

#### 12.1.7 Variations and combinations with repetition (introduction)
`[Block: Numbers and Operations > Counting: combinatorics techniques]`
Formulas $VR_{n,k} = n^k$ and $CR_{n,k} = \binom{n+k-1}{k}$. Examples: passwords, distributing identical resources.

### 12.2 Introduction to probability

#### 12.2.1 Random experiment, sample space and event

#### 12.2.2 Algebra of events: union, intersection, complement
`[Block: Statistics > Probability: sample space, algebra of events]`

### 12.3 Conditional probability and independence

#### 12.3.1 Conditional probability: definition and calculation
`[Block: Statistics > Conditional probability, Kolmogorov, Venn]`
$P(A|B) = P(A \cap B)/P(B)$. Interpretation: updating probabilities with partial information. Applications: analysis of conditional socioeconomic data.

#### 12.3.2 Multiplication rule: probability of the intersection
`[Block: Statistics > Conditional probability, Kolmogorov, Venn]`
$P(A \cap B) = P(B) \cdot P(A|B)$. Generalized multiplication rule for $n$ events.

#### 12.3.3 Independent events: definition and criterion
`[Block: Statistics > Conditional probability, Kolmogorov, Venn]`
Definition: $P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B)$. Independence vs. mutual exclusivity. Examples in opinion polls and surveys.

#### 12.3.4 Tree diagrams for compound probabilities
`[Block: Statistics > Total probability and Bayes, tree diagrams]`
Building probability trees. Multiplying probabilities along branches. Applications: probability of demographic profiles, sampling.

#### 12.3.5 Law of total probability
`[Block: Statistics > Total probability and Bayes, tree diagrams]`
Partition of $\Omega$. Total probability formula. Applications: risk analysis with population segmentation.

#### 12.3.6 Bayes' theorem: posterior probability
`[Block: Statistics > Total probability and Bayes, tree diagrams]`
Bayes' formula. Interpretation: updating probability in light of new evidence. Applications: reliability of diagnostic tests, analysis of stratified surveys.

### 12.4 Random variables and distributions

#### 12.4.1 Discrete random variable: definition, probability mass function and distribution function
`[Block: Statistics > Discrete and continuous random variables]`
Formal definition. Probability mass function $P(X = x_i)$. Cumulative distribution function $F(x)$. Examples: number of correct answers in a test.

#### 12.4.2 Expected value and variance of a discrete random variable
`[Block: Statistics > Discrete and continuous random variables]`
$E(X) = \sum x_i P(X = x_i)$. $Var(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2$. Properties. Applications: expected value of a public policy, expected cost.

#### 12.4.3 Continuous random variable: density function and cumulative distribution (introduction)
`[Block: Statistics > Discrete and continuous random variables]`

#### 12.4.4 Binomial distribution: Bernoulli trial, parameters and probability mass function
`[Block: Statistics > Binomial and normal distributions]`
Conditions for a binomial model ($n$ independent trials, constant probability $p$). $X \sim B(n, p)$. $P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k}p^k(1-p)^{n-k}$. Applications: studying population proportions (voting intention, satisfaction).

#### 12.4.5 Expected value and variance of the binomial; probability calculations
`[Block: Statistics > Binomial and normal distributions]`
$E(X) = np$, $Var(X) = np(1-p)$. Computing exact and cumulative probabilities. Calculator or table use.

#### 12.4.6 Normal distribution: parameters, Gaussian bell curve and symmetry properties
`[Block: Statistics > Binomial and normal distributions]`
$X \sim N(\mu, \sigma)$. Bell-curve shape: symmetric around $\mu$. Role of $\mu$ (translation) and $\sigma$ (width). Empirical rule 68-95-99.7. Applications: income distribution (approximation), test scores, measurement errors.

#### 12.4.7 Standardization and the standard normal distribution N(0,1): using tables
`[Block: Statistics > Binomial and normal distributions]`
Standardization $Z = (X - \mu)/\sigma$. Using the standard normal table $\Phi(z)$. Calculating $P(X < a)$, $P(X > a)$, $P(a < X < b)$.

#### 12.4.8 Normal approximation to the binomial: condition and continuity correction
`[Block: Statistics > Normal approximation to the binomial]`
Condition: $np \geq 5$ and $n(1-p) \geq 5$. Continuity correction (±0.5). Examples with electoral poll data.
